Current Exhibition // Trace

Trace brings together a series of works that examine the subtle, often overlooked structures that shape human experience—whether personal, intergenerational, or spatial. Across books, performances, and site-specific interventions, the exhibition investigates how memory, routine, and environmental rhythm leave imprints on our lives, and how individuals navigate the forces that choreograph movement, meaning, and identity.
Spanning intimate autobiography, collective oral history, creative process, and urban behavior, each piece foregrounds the act of noticing. Dear Dad follows a young woman’s emergence into adulthood amid caregiving and loss, revealing how familial responsibilities quietly reorient a life. Trace, the exhibition’s namesake, intertwines the stories of three women born between 1920 and 1930, illustrating how personal narratives—marked by immigration, war, segregation, and resilience—form a broader intergenerational tapestry. 352 Pathways turns inward, documenting a designer’s search for clarity within the nonlinear terrain of creative practice, where questions of perfection, process, and self-understanding play out through accumulated fragments.

In the performance works, this attention shifts to the infrastructures that shape collective behavior. Sidewalk Sound amplifies the latent rhythms embedded in urban architecture, transforming everyday pedestrian movement into a live sonic composition. Traffic: An Intersection of Time and Space disrupts a familiar public walkway to reveal how individuals interpret, adapt to, and derive meaning from subtle changes in their environment.
Together, these works articulate a central premise: that our lives are formed not only by monumental events but by the quiet patterns, inherited histories, and immediate surroundings that guide how we move through the world. Trace invites viewers to consider the marks we carry forward, the ones we leave behind, and the systems—visible and invisible—that connect them.
